Degree

The degree of a polynomial function is the highest exponent x is raised to in the polynomial’s monomial parts.

Degree Polynomial
4 2x^4 + 3x
3 3x^2 - 2x^3

Factoring

Factoring a polynomial is the process of breaking it down into irreducible factors, that is factors that cannot themselves be factored. For polynomial functions determining the values of x such that y = 0, gives the factors of the function.

Factoring can either be done through the use of a formula, such as the quadratic, quartic or quitic formula, although this becomes arbitrarily complex as the degree of the polynomial increases.

Newton's Method

Because as the order of the polynomial increases, the computational viability of its corresponding root equation falls drastically, there are other methods of computing a function’s root.

The most effective amongst these is referred to as Newton’s Method or sometimes the Newton-Raphson Method. This method uses a series of successive approximations of x that generally become closer to a function's root x values, as the approximation number increases.

The approximations of x can be described as

x_1 = x_0 - (f(x_0) / f'(x_0)) x_(n + 1) = x_n - (f(x_n) / f'(x_n))

Quadratics

Quadratic are polynomials of degree 2. Quadratic can have either 0, 1 or 2 possible intercepts. Additionally because quadratics are symmetric, they have an axis of symmetry. They can be expressed in a number of forms.

Standard Form y = ax^2 + bx + c
Vertex Form y = (a - x)^2 + k
Factored Form y = a(x - p)(x - q)

Polynomial Long Division

Polynomial long division is the algorithm used to divide one polynomial by another of equal or lesser degree.

Polynomial Factor Theorem

The polynomial factor theorem is a theorem connecting zeroes and factors of a polynomial.

The theorem states if f(x) has some factor (x - k) tham f(k) must be 0 and k is a root.

Smoothstep

Smoothstep is an extremely valuable 3rd order polynomial sigmoid. It maps any x value from 0 to 1 to a corresponding y value from 0 to 1 in an S shape. This is of significant importance in several computationally related tasks that involve slowly transitioning between two values.

Example

The acceleration of a shipping truck carrying potatos over the course of its journey can be described by the polynomial a(h) = 3(3x^2 - 2x^3).

Determine the acceleration of the truck at h = 0.8

a(0.8) = 3(3(0.8)^2 - 2(0.8)^3) = 3(3(0.64) - 2(0.512)) = 9(0.64) - 6(0.512) = 5.76 - 3.072 = 2.688
